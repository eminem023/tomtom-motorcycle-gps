How to Update TomTom Motorcycle GPS Maps

TomTom's motorcycle GPS devices like the TomTom Rider series are built for adventure, offering rugged durability and ride-specific routing. To ensure a smooth and accurate ride, it's vital to keep the maps and firmware updated. This guide walks you through the steps to update your motorcycle GPS device.

Why Update Your Motorcycle GPS?

  • Get the latest routes, road data, and traffic patterns
  • Access new scenic routes and winding road options
  • Enhance GPS speed, accuracy, and performance
  • Fix known software bugs and stability issues

Steps to Update via Wi-Fi

  • Turn on your TomTom Rider or compatible motorcycle GPS
  • Connect to a reliable Wi-Fi network
  • Go to Menu > Settings > Updates & New Items
  • Select available updates, including maps and system
  • Tap Update Now and wait for installation

Update with MyDrive Connect (Computer)

  • Download and install MyDrive Connect on your PC or Mac
  • Connect the GPS to your computer with a USB cable
  • Login to your TomTom account
  • Follow on-screen instructions to download and install updates

Tips for Reliable Updating

  • Keep your GPS plugged into power while updating
  • Use a fast and stable internet connection
  • Do not disconnect the device during installation

Common Problems and Fixes

  • Slow downloads: Use a faster internet connection or update during off-peak hours
  • Wi-Fi fails: Restart both your router and device
  • Not enough space: Insert a microSD card if supported

Keeping your TomTom Motorcycle GPS updated means smoother rides, more accurate routes, and the ability to explore without worry. Whether you're heading out on a Sunday cruise or a long-distance trip, up-to-date maps ensure you're always on track.
